These are tools and resources that have helped me as I've tried to teach myself how to make music

LMMS
LMMS is a free, open-source digital audio workstation. It provides everything you need to start making music in a single download. The focus is on simple workflows all within one application, though it can also be configured to handle more complicated setups.
Audacity
Audacity is a free, open-source audio editor. It's great for working with vocals or samples and also for polishing pieces up after the main composition is done.
Soundfonts
Synths are fun to play with and can produce a staggering array of different sounds, but nothing can ever quite beat the warmth and life of real instruments. If you haven't got any to record yourself, a good compromise are Soundfonts, which are created from samples of real instruments. Here's a list of some that are freely available. Here's a site that catalogues samples in the higher-quality GIG format.
